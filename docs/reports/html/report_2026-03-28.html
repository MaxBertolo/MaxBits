IndexCache, a new sparse attention optimizer, delivers 1.82x faster inference on long-context AI models

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: IndexCache is a novel sparse attention optimizer developed by researchers at Tsinghua University and Z.ai, designed to significantly accelerate inference on long-context AI models.
  • Who: Researchers at Tsinghua University and Z.ai developed IndexCache, a technique to optimize large language model performance and reduce computational costs.
  • What it does: IndexCache optimizes DeepSeek Sparse Attention models by reusing token indices across layers, drastically reducing redundant computation and speeding up both prefill and decoding phases.
  • Why it matters: This innovation delivers up to 1.82x faster inference and 1.48x higher generation throughput for long-context LLMs, translating directly into reduced operational costs and improved user experience.
  • Strategic view: IndexCache enables Telco, Media, and Tech companies to deploy more performant and cost-efficient long-context AI applications, enhancing competitive advantage in advanced LLM capabilities and service delivery.
