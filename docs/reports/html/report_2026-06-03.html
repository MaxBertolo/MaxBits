Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Plus supports text, video and imagery inputs at low cost of $0.4/$1.6 per 1M token — but it's proprietary

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Plus is a newly released, multimodal large language model that processes text, video, and imagery at a significantly low operational cost.
  • Who: Alibaba, through its AI division and Alibaba Cloud, developed and now offers this advanced Qwen3.7-Plus model under a proprietary commercial license.
  • What it does: This model provides multimodal capabilities for tasks like enterprise-grade visual creation and video analysis, alongside an impressive 1-million token context window with reasoning state preservation.
  • Why it matters: The model's low cost and strong multimodal performance are significant, but Alibaba's shift to a proprietary license marks a departure from its prior open-source strategy, disappointing some users.
  • Strategic view: Alibaba's move to monetize advanced multimodal AI through proprietary, cost-effective models signals its intent to capture market share and deepen cloud platform integration for enterprise clients.

Perplexity AI unveils hybrid local-cloud inference system at Computex 2026

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Perplexity AI unveiled a hybrid local-server inference orchestrator that autonomously routes AI workloads between a user's device and cloud-based frontier models in real-time.
  • Who: Perplexity AI, a $20 billion search startup, demonstrated this system at Computex 2026 alongside Intel, impacting major chipmakers like Nvidia and Intel.
  • What it does: This system intelligently decides where each part of an AI task should execute, keeping sensitive data local while sending heavier reasoning tasks to the cloud for processing.
  • Why it matters: It balances intelligence, accuracy, privacy, and cost by optimizing workload execution, addressing enterprise data governance concerns, and reducing cloud reliance for sensitive tasks.
  • Strategic view: This technology shifts AI compute economics towards powerful on-device silicon, potentially reducing the urgency for massive sovereign data centers, and elevates orchestration over individual models as a key strategic differentiator.

The Agentic Reckoning: Enterprise AI organizations have a runtime problem, not a model problem — and most are building the wrong solution

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: This research highlights that enterprise AI agent deployments struggle primarily with fragile runtime infrastructure, not model intelligence, hindering successful production operations and scalability.
  • Who: The findings target C-level executives and technology leaders across large to mid-sized enterprises in diverse sectors, who are actively deploying or planning AI agent solutions.
  • What it does: Fragile AI runtimes lead to significant engineering overhead, escalating costs, context loss, and compounding errors, preventing AI agents from scaling reliably or delivering expected business value in production.
  • Why it matters: This insight reveals a critical need to shift enterprise AI investment from just model capabilities to robust runtime infrastructure, otherwise, agentic initiatives face widespread failure, mirroring past automation pitfalls.
  • Strategic view: Executives must prioritize investment in resilient, state-aware AI agent runtime platforms to ensure operational success, reduce engineering waste, and secure the long-term return on their significant AI technology investments.
