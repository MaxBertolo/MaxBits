Brex built its AI agent policy by watching what agents actually do, not by writing rules first

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Brex developed CrabTrap, an open-source HTTP/HTTPS proxy that intercepts agent network traffic, using policy rules and an LLM-as-a-judge to enforce security and control.
  • Who: Brex, a financial technology company, created CrabTrap as an open-source platform to enable secure and controlled deployment of autonomous AI agents in enterprise settings.
  • What it does: CrabTrap enforces AI agent policy at the network transport layer by observing real-time behavior, learning dynamically, and adapting rules to approve or deny requests.
  • Why it matters: It matters because CrabTrap provides a novel, centralized approach to securely govern AI agents by balancing their capabilities with necessary safety protocols at scale, overcoming traditional limitations.
  • Strategic view: This method offers TMT companies a scalable model for securely deploying autonomous AI agents enterprise-wide, boosting organizational confidence and mitigating security and operational risks by learning from behavior.
