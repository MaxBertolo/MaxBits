AWS launches Kiro powers with Stripe, Figma, and Datadog integrations for AI-assisted coding

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: AWS Kiro powers is a system enabling AI coding assistants to gain instant, specialized expertise in specific development tools and workflows, like Stripe and Figma.
  • Who: Amazon Web Services (AWS) launched Kiro powers, partnering with nine technology companies including Datadog, Figma, and Stripe, at its re:Invent conference.
  • What it does: It dynamically loads specialized tool definitions and best practices only when needed, reducing computational resource consumption, improving AI agent efficiency, and lowering costs.
  • Why it matters: This innovation addresses "context rot," a key bottleneck where AI coding assistants become overwhelmed and costly by loading too many irrelevant tool definitions upfront.
  • Strategic view: AWS is democratizing advanced AI development practices and expanding its "agentic AI" ecosystem by offering a cost-efficient alternative to fine-tuning, enhancing developer productivity and platform lock-in.

Inside NetSuite’s next act: Evan Goldberg on the future of AI-powered business systems

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: NetSuite Next is a significant product evolution, embedding contextual, conversational, and agentic AI directly into business workflows to act as an extension of operations rather than separate tools.
  • Who: Oracle NetSuite, led by founder Evan Goldberg, is innovating enterprise software by deeply integrating advanced AI capabilities into its unified platform.
  • What it does: NetSuite Next autonomously executes operations like account reconciliation and cash flow prediction, providing context-aware intelligence and collaborative workflow design directly within existing business processes.
  • Why it matters: This matters because it fundamentally shifts enterprise AI from bolted-on tools to deeply embedded, autonomous intelligence within core workflows, driving operational efficiency and deeper insights for businesses.
  • Strategic view: This signals a strategic shift towards deeply integrated, native AI within core enterprise platforms, redefining competitive advantage through enhanced automation, security, and intelligent decision-making, setting a new bar for business system evolution.

Gong study: Sales teams using AI generate 77% more revenue per rep

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: A new study by Gong highlights that sales teams leveraging AI generate significantly more revenue per representative, signaling a dramatic shift in enterprise trust and adoption of AI for revenue generation.
  • Who: Revenue intelligence company Gong conducted a comprehensive study, analyzing sales opportunities across 3,600+ companies and surveying 3,000+ global revenue leaders, on AI's impact.
  • What it does: This research demonstrates that organizations embedding AI into core go-to-market strategies achieve 65% higher win rates and 77% more revenue per sales representative compared to non-users.
  • Why it matters: As revenue growth decelerates and sales quota attainment falls, AI boosts existing team productivity, shifting executive priorities towards maximizing output from current sales talent.
  • Strategic view: TMT executives must strategically invest in domain-specific AI for revenue operations to enhance forecasting accuracy, drive sales productivity, and reshape roles, securing a competitive edge in a slowing growth environment.
