VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

The agent evaluation gap: Enterprise AI organizations have a reality-alignment problem, not a coverage problem — and most are shipping to production anyway

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Enterprise AI organizations face a critical "evaluation gap" where autonomous agents are deployed with increasing trust in their capabilities, despite widespread distrust in their automated testing.
  • Who: Technical leaders across 157 mid-market to large enterprises are grappling with the paradox of deploying AI agents with insufficient testing assurance.
  • What it does: Half of companies have deployed AI agents that passed internal evaluations but subsequently failed customers, demonstrating a disconnect between testing and real-world performance.
  • Why it matters: This trend accelerates the deployment of potentially faulty AI systems, increasing customer-facing failures and posing significant reputational and operational risks to organizations.
  • Strategic view: Executives must prioritize robust, real-world aligned AI evaluation frameworks and governance to mitigate escalating risks associated with autonomous agent deployment and maintain customer trust.

The AI compute gap: Enterprises are buying infrastructure faster than they can measure what it costs

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Enterprises are experiencing an "AI compute gap," rapidly investing in AI infrastructure without adequate visibility into costs or optimal utilization.
  • Who: The study targets 107 enterprises, showing current reliance on hyperscalers but strong intent to evaluate and switch to specialized AI cloud providers.
