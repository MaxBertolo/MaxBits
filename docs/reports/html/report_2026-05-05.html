Microsoft takes Agent 365 out of preview as shadow AI becomes an enterprise threat

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Microsoft's Agent 365, now generally available, is a unified management platform designed to observe, govern, and secure AI agents across diverse enterprise environments.
  • Who: Microsoft launched Agent 365 for C-level executives and IT/security teams in Telco, Media, and Tech, aiming to address the urgent operational challenges of autonomous AI.
  • What it does: It discovers and manages AI agents, including 'shadow AI' on employee devices and those on third-party clouds, providing a control plane for governance, security, and policy enforcement.
  • Why it matters: This platform is crucial because the rapid proliferation of autonomous AI agents, especially 'shadow AI,' presents significant and immediate security, data leakage, and governance risks to enterprises.
  • Strategic view: Agent 365 positions Microsoft as a critical enabler of AI adoption by providing essential governance, mitigating enterprise risks, and setting a standard for managing the evolving, distributed AI agent ecosystem.

The RAG era is ending for agentic AI — a new compilation-stage knowledge layer is what comes next

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Pinecone's Nexus is a new knowledge engine designed to optimize how agentic AI systems access and utilize enterprise data, moving beyond traditional RAG limitations.
  • Who: Pinecone, a pioneering vector database company, launched Nexus and KnowQL, signaling a strategic pivot to address the evolving needs of advanced agentic AI deployments.
  • What it does: Nexus features a context compiler, composable retriever, and KnowQL, which together pre-process raw data into structured knowledge artifacts, significantly reducing AI agent token costs and improving reasoning accuracy.
  • Why it matters: This shift from runtime interpretation to compilation-stage knowledge creation is crucial for enterprises seeking scalable, auditable, and cost-effective AI agents with consistent, deterministic outcomes.
  • Strategic view: TMT executives must recognize this architectural evolution as a foundational shift for enterprise AI, moving towards more intelligent, efficient, and compliant agent workflows across their digital estates.

Inside AMEX’s agentic commerce stack: How intent contracts and single-use tokens enforce AI transactions

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: American Express is creating an Agentic Commerce Experiences (ACE) developer kit that allows AI agents to shop and pay for users within its payment network.
  • Who: American Express, acting as both card issuer and payment network, is the key player building this system, also engaging in broader agentic commerce protocols.
  • What it does: The ACE kit provides agent registration, intent contracts, single-use payment tokens, and cart validation, ensuring secure, controlled, and verifiable AI-driven e-commerce transactions.
  • Why it matters: This system matters by addressing crucial trust, security, and accountability issues in agentic commerce, enforcing user intent and transaction boundaries to prevent fraud effectively.
  • Strategic view: Amex's initiative strategically positions it to define foundational trust and payment layers within the nascent agentic commerce ecosystem, influencing future industry standards for AI-driven transactions.
