Morgan Stanley cut its riskiest reconciliation job in half — by making its agents less autonomous

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Morgan Stanley deployed FIXR, an internal agentic AI system designed to assist with critical profit and loss reconciliation by keeping humans tightly in the loop.
  • Who: Morgan Stanley, through its Managing Director Todd Johnson, implemented the FIXR system, integrating human controllers to oversee and refine its AI-driven processes.
  • What it does: The FIXR system automatically analyzes profit and loss "breaks," proposes resolutions based on learned rules, and then incorporates human controller feedback to iteratively improve its accuracy and automation.
  • Why it matters: This approach matters because it significantly reduces time spent on critical financial reconciliation, freeing up human controllers for higher-value analytical work by prioritizing a human-in-the-loop, learning-driven automation strategy.
  • Strategic view: This case strategically demonstrates that a human-in-the-loop, process-first AI deployment focused on iterative learning and accountability can achieve significant efficiency, offering a sustainable model for complex enterprise workflows.
