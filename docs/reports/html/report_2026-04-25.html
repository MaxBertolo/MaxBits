85% of enterprises are running AI agents. Only 5% trust them enough to ship.

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Enterprises face a critical trust deficit: while 85% pilot AI agents, only 5% deploy them to production due to security concerns over "action risk" from autonomous operations.
  • Who: Cisco, partnering with Nvidia, is addressing this challenge by developing robust security architectures for AI agents and enforcing an aggressive internal mandate for AI-driven software development.
  • What it does: Cisco’s Defense Claw, integrated with Nvidia's OpenShell, offers an open-source framework for embedding security policies into AI agent workflows from inception, enhancing trust and accelerating deployment.
  • Why it matters: This trust gap is slowing enterprise AI adoption, underscoring the urgent need for robust security frameworks to transition from pilot programs to scalable, business-critical AI agent deployments.
  • Strategic view: C-levels must prioritize developing comprehensive AI trust architectures and embrace AI-driven engineering to achieve market dominance, as trusted AI agent delegation will define future operational efficiency and innovation.

DeepSeek-V4 arrives with near state-of-the-art intelligence at 1/6th the cost of Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: DeepSeek-V4 is a new 1.6-trillion-parameter open-source Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) AI model that offers near state-of-the-art performance comparable to leading closed-source systems.
  • Who: DeepSeek is a Chinese AI startup, an offshoot of High-Flyer Capital Management, known for its powerful open-source models challenging proprietary U.S. giants.
  • What it does: DeepSeek-V4 delivers near state-of-the-art AI intelligence, on par with top proprietary models, at approximately one-sixth the cost of leading competitors like GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.7.
  • Why it matters: This release matters because it dramatically lowers the cost of accessing frontier-class AI, making advanced automation economically viable for a much wider range of enterprise applications.
  • Strategic view: This intensifies competitive pressure on premium AI providers, shifting focus from pure performance to cost-efficiency and accelerating enterprise AI adoption across the TMT sector globally.

CVSS scored these two Palo Alto CVEs as manageable. Chained, they gave attackers root access to 13,000 devices.

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Traditional CVSS vulnerability scoring systems are failing to accurately assess real-world risk, leading to significant security gaps when adversaries chain individually 'manageable' vulnerabilities or exploit other vectors.
  • Who: Attackers exploited chained Palo Alto Network vulnerabilities, while traditional CVSS scoring by NVD and Palo Alto failed to properly assess the combined severe risk to 13,000 exposed devices.
  • What it does: It exposes how individually low-scored Palo Alto Network vulnerabilities, when chained together, granted attackers root access to over 13,000 devices, bypassing standard patch prioritization and exposing systemic security weaknesses.
  • Why it matters: This failure matters because it demonstrates the severe limitations of CVSS-first vulnerability management, leading to critical breaches by sophisticated adversaries who exploit chained vulnerabilities and non-CVE attack vectors.
  • Strategic view: TMT executives must urgently shift from CVSS-centric vulnerability management to comprehensive, context-aware risk prioritization models that anticipate chained exploits, non-technical attack vectors, and AI-accelerated threat landscapes.
