Intuit is betting its 40 years of small business data can outlast the SaaSpocalypse

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: The 'SaaSpocalypse' refers to a market re-evaluation where established SaaS companies are seeing stock declines as agentic AI tools threaten to automate tasks, offering outcomes over software access.
  • Who: Intuit, along with other major SaaS players like IBM and Adobe, faces this challenge from agentic AI developers such as Anthropic, leveraging tools like Claude Cowork.
  • What it does: These advanced AI agents automate complex business functions, including bookkeeping and tax preparation, directly performing tasks previously requiring human interaction with traditional SaaS applications.
  • Why it matters: This shift fundamentally impacts SaaS business models by moving from per-seat software subscriptions to outcome-based services, potentially commoditizing existing applications and decreasing market capitalization.
  • Strategic view: Intuit's strategy emphasizes its proprietary data and deep customer understanding as a competitive moat, while proactively partnering with Anthropic to embed agentic AI capabilities within its platforms.
