MeMo's memory model lets teams upgrade their LLM without retraining it — and performance jumps 26%

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: MeMo is a novel AI framework that introduces a modular architecture enabling Large Language Models to acquire and update knowledge continuously without costly full retraining.
  • Who: Developed by university researchers, MeMo is intended for enterprise AI developers and organizations seeking to enhance their Large Language Models with continuous, cost-effective knowledge updates.
  • What it does: MeMo separates knowledge storage in a smaller memory model from an executive LLM's reasoning, allowing continuous updates and avoiding issues like context limits and catastrophic forgetting.
  • Why it matters: This innovation matters by significantly reducing the cost and complexity of maintaining up-to-date knowledge in enterprise LLMs, enabling continuous learning and improved performance.
  • Strategic view: For TMT executives, MeMo offers a strategic advantage by significantly lowering AI operational costs and accelerating LLM adaptation to new data without retraining, fostering enterprise innovation.

The AI agent bottleneck isn't model performance — it's permissions

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Workday's Sana system addresses the critical bottleneck of AI agent adoption, which is permissions and governance, by leveraging its existing system of record as the authoritative control layer for agent actions.
  • Who: Workday, in partnership with Google through Gemini Enterprise, is a primary player, alongside other enterprise software providers and third-party identity verification services.
  • What it does: Workday's Sana integrates Google Gemini for conversational AI, then uses Workday's security model to authenticate users and authorize agents to perform actions precisely within their specific organizational permissions.

  • Strategic view: For C-levels, this signifies that successful enterprise AI agent deployment hinges on robust governance and integration with existing systems of record, not merely advanced model performance, defining a critical competitive advantage.
