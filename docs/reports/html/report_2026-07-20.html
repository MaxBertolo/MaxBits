Capital One releases VulnHunter, an open-source AI tool that finds software flaws before hackers do

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Capital One released VulnHunter, an open-source, agentic AI security tool that scans source code for exploitable vulnerabilities, maps attack paths, and proposes fixes pre-production.
  • Who: Capital One, a major financial institution, developed and open-sourced this tool, currently running on Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 model within a Claude Code environment.
  • What it does: VulnHunter uniquely employs an "attacker-first forward analysis" and a "falsification engine" to accurately identify and explain exploit paths, significantly reducing false positives for developers.
  • Why it matters: This initiative responds to the rapidly evolving AI threat landscape, offering a public defensive resource to combat sophisticated AI attacks and improve software supply chain security for all.
  • Strategic view: Capital One's open-sourcing demonstrates a strategic shift towards collective cybersecurity defense and proactive risk management, fostering industry-wide collaboration against advanced AI threats.

Brex built its AI agent policy by watching what agents actually do, not by writing rules first

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Brex's CrabTrap is an open-source HTTP/HTTPS proxy platform designed to intercept and control network traffic from AI agents, enforcing policy decisions with an LLM-as-a-judge mechanism.
  • Who: Brex, a financial technology firm, developed this internal platform and open-sourced it to safely deploy and manage autonomous AI agents within its enterprise operations.
  • What it does: CrabTrap intercepts all AI agent network requests, applies a combination of static and LLM-based policy rules, approving or denying them to enhance security and expand agent utility.
  • Why it matters: It shifts AI agent governance to a centralized network control plane, offering a more robust and scalable solution than existing SDK or model-specific guardrails, improving enterprise confidence.
  • Strategic view: This approach enables Telco, Media, and Tech companies to confidently deploy powerful AI agents by providing an essential, framework-agnostic security and governance layer for safe enterprise integration.

China’s Moonshot AI releases Kimi K3, the largest open-source model ever, rivaling top U.S. systems

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Kimi K3 is a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-source large language model, now the world's largest, rivaling top proprietary systems like OpenAI's and Anthropic's.
  • Who: Moonshot AI, a Beijing-based artificial intelligence startup backed by Alibaba, developed and released Kimi K3, demonstrating a significant comeback in the competitive global AI landscape.
  • What it does: Kimi K3 offers frontier-class LLM performance with a 1-million-token context window, visual understanding, and autonomous reasoning, designed for complex, long-horizon tasks like chip design.
  • Why it matters: This release marks a watershed for open-source AI, closing the performance gap with leading proprietary models and escalating the global AI race, particularly from China.
  • Strategic view: TMT executives must recognize Kimi K3 as a potent, accessible open-source option accelerating AI innovation globally, demanding re-evaluation of current platform strategies and competitive landscapes.
