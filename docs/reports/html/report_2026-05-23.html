Your AI agents need a terminal, not just a vector database

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI) is a new technique that allows AI agents to search raw data using command-line tools, bypassing traditional vector databases.
  • Who: Researchers at multiple universities developed Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI), a novel approach enabling AI agents to search raw data directly using standard terminal tools.
  • What it does: DCI allows AI agents to dynamically interact with current, raw data, formulating hypotheses and executing precise lexical searches for multi-step tasks that traditional retrieval misses.
  • Why it matters: It matters because traditional RAG systems often bottleneck agents by providing limited, stale, or semantically misaligned information, hindering complex, multi-step reasoning over dynamic enterprise data.
  • Strategic view: This approach significantly enhances enterprise AI by enabling more reliable and accurate agentic workflows for dynamic data, critical for use cases like debugging, compliance, and multi-document analysis.

Valid certificates, stolen accounts: how attackers broke npm's last trust signal

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: A series of sophisticated attacks compromised developer tools and AI agent frameworks by exploiting stolen credentials and fundamental security verification flaws in the software supply chain.
  • Who: Financially motivated threat actors, including TeamPCP and STARDUST CHOLLIMA, are targeting software supply chains and developer identities across various platforms, including npm and AI coding CLIs.
  • What it does: Attackers are forging software provenance, leveraging auto-execution vulnerabilities in AI coding tools, and stealing critical credentials, leading to the injection of malicious code into widely used development environments.
  • Why it matters: The compromised integrity of developer tools and AI agents bypasses traditional security, exposing sensitive corporate data, intellectual property, and increasing significant operational risks for enterprises.
  • Strategic view: C-level leaders must urgently prioritize re-architecting software supply chain security and enforcing stringent identity management across all developer and AI tools to protect critical enterprise assets.

D&B's database of 642 million businesses was built for humans, not AI agents. So they rebuilt it.

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Dun & Bradstreet rebuilt its 180-year-old, 642 million-business commercial database, traditionally designed for human analysts, to be compatible with and efficiently queryable by AI agents.
  • Who: Dun & Bradstreet, led by its Chief Data and Analytics Officer, rebuilt its Commercial Graph to enable AI agents used by its nearly 200,000 global customers to efficiently query and utilize its vast business data.
  • What it does: D&B consolidated its fragmented data into a unified, cloud-based knowledge graph, developed a structured access layer with entity resolution for agents, and implemented "Know Your Agent" identity and workflow consistency checks.
  • Why it matters: This rebuild matters because legacy data architectures, designed for human interaction, are fundamentally incompatible with AI agents' demands for real-time querying, dynamic relationships, and verified entity consistency, a challenge many enterprises face.
  • Strategic view: C-level executives must prioritize re-architecting legacy data foundations for AI agents, focusing on unified, dynamic knowledge graphs, agent identity management, and inherent data lineage to unlock AI's potential and maintain competitive relevance.
