Databricks' OfficeQA uncovers disconnect: AI agents ace abstract tests but stall at 45% on enterprise docs

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Databricks' OfficeQA is a new benchmark designed to assess AI agent performance on complex, document-heavy enterprise tasks, utilizing real-world datasets like US Treasury Bulletins.
  • Who: Databricks, a data and AI platform company, developed OfficeQA to test leading AI agents like Claude Opus and GPT-5.1 on practical enterprise document workloads.
  • What it does: OfficeQA evaluates AI agents' "grounded reasoning" by requiring them to answer complex questions based on real-world enterprise documents, exposing critical limitations in parsing, versioning, and visual interpretation.
  • Why it matters: It matters because existing AI benchmarks don't reflect enterprise reality, and OfficeQA reveals current top AI agents achieve only 45-68% accuracy on practical document tasks, exposing a critical performance gap.
  • Strategic view: This benchmark mandates that executives recalibrate AI strategies, prioritizing practical document intelligence solutions and robust data preparation, as current AI agents significantly underperform on critical enterprise tasks, despite abstract successes.
