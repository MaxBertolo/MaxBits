Nvidia launches enterprise AI agent platform with Adobe, Salesforce, SAP among 17 adopters at GTC 2026

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Nvidia launched the Agent Toolkit, an open-source platform designed for enterprises to build and deploy autonomous AI agents.
  • Who: Nvidia introduced its Agent Toolkit, a platform adopted by 17 major enterprise software companies like Adobe, Salesforce, and SAP, to power their next-generation AI products.
  • What it does: The toolkit provides a unified platform with models, runtime, security, and optimization libraries, enabling secure, autonomous AI agent operation and simplifying enterprise AI development.
  • Why it matters: This platform unifies enterprise AI agent development, significantly lowers adoption barriers, and fundamentally ties future AI software growth to Nvidia's optimized hardware infrastructure.
  • Strategic view: Nvidia is strategically positioning itself as the indispensable foundational "tollbooth" for the rapidly expanding enterprise AI agent ecosystem, ensuring sustained demand for its GPU hardware.

Microsoft launches 3 new AI models in direct shot at OpenAI and Google

VentureBeat – AI · Topic: AI/Cloud/Quantum

  • What it is: Microsoft launched three new foundational AI models, MAI-Transcribe-1, MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2, developed in-house and available via Microsoft Foundry and MAI Playground.
  • Who: Microsoft, aiming for AI self-sufficiency, developed these models to directly compete with leading AI labs like OpenAI and Google in foundational model development.
  • What it does: These models offer state-of-the-art speech-to-text, realistic voice generation, and enhanced image creation, performing competitively while requiring fewer computational resources.
  • Why it matters: This launch signals Microsoft's strategic shift toward AI self-sufficiency, enabling independent superintelligence pursuits and reducing reliance on external partners for foundational models.
  • Strategic view: This intensifies foundational AI competition, drives vertical integration among tech giants, and underscores the imperative for TMT players to control core AI intellectual property.
